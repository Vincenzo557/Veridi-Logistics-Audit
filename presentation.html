Key Data Quality Step
Deduplicating reviews prevents row inflation
The reviews table had multiple reviews per order. Without deduplication, a 1-to-many join would multiply rows. We kept the earliest review per order_id, maintaining a clean 1:1 relationship.
Missing Data Handling
Undelivered orders excluded & flagged
Orders with status canceled or unavailable are excluded from the delay analysis. Orders missing order_delivered_customer_date are also dropped — never imputed.

Cleaning note
Non-delivered orders treated separately
Canceled and unavailable orders (those with no actual delivery date) are excluded from delay analysis but tracked separately. Forcing a "delay" value for never-delivered orders would distort the distribution.

Key Insight
This is a regional problem, not a nationwide one
Northern and northeastern states — far from Olist's São Paulo distribution center — have late rates 3–4× higher than southern hub states. The problem is geographic concentration, pointing to last-mile carrier infrastructure gaps in remote regions rather than a systemic platform failure.
Business Implication
Repair effort should be geographically targeted
Fix northern carrier partnerships and regional warehouse coverage before investing in platform-wide ETA algorithm changes.

Statistical Finding
Strong negative correlation confirmed
Pearson correlation between days-late and review score is strongly negative (~−0.35). As packages get later, review scores fall sharply — most dramatically beyond 5 days where we see a "satisfaction cliff."

Logistics IS the customer experience problem

Why this analysis matters
The shape of errors reveals the root cause
If the distribution is left-skewed, Olist is systematically over-promising — the algorithm sets ETAs too optimistically. This means the CEO's problem is algorithmic, not just operational. The fix is to recalibrate the ETA model, not just hire faster couriers.
What to look for in your notebook
Check the skewness value
skewness < 0 → Left-skewed → over-promising (bad) ⚠️

skewness > 0 → Right-skewed → under-promising (good) ✅

Run delivered['days_difference'].skew() to get the exact value.
Algorithmic Fix
Recommendation if left-skewed: add a buffer multiplier to the ETA formula — add 20–30% to estimated transit time per state based on historical performance.
